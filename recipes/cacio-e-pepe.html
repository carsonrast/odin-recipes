Cacio E Pepe


Description

Cacio e Pepe is a classic Roman dish consisting of only 3 ingredients. It's name literally translates to "cheese and pepper." A little technique is required, but with practice, you can make your own super creamy pasta that explodes with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese*
  • 1/2 lb. dried spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns

*other hard, nutty cheeses will work, such as Parmeggiano Reggiano, but classic Cacio e Pepe uses only Pecorino Pomano, which is a very flavorful sheep's milk cheese.

  1. Bring 2 qt of water to a boil.
  2. While waiting on the water to boil, in a hot skillet, toast the peppercorns until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Place peppercorns in a pepper mill for grinding later.
  3. Once water is boiling, add in the pasta. Cook according to box instructions for al dente.
  4. One minute before pasta reaches al dente, use tongs to remove it from the boiling water and place in a skillet over medium-low heat. It's ok to not drain the pasta - the pasta water is needed for bringing the sauce together.
  5. Add about 1/8 cup of the pasta water to the pan and swirl to bring the temperature down. Add in about a third of the grated cheese and immediately toss vigorously.
  6. Continue to add the remaining cheese and stir continuously until the sauce thickens slightly. If the sauce becomes too thick, add a little more pasta water.
  7. Grind in the black pepper and stir.
  8. Serve on a pre-warmed plate and eat right away - the sauce will start to stiffen in about 15 minutes.
